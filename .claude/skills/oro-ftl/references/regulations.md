## 1. Scope and Definitions

### ORO.FTL.100 — Scope
This Subpart establishes requirements to be met by an operator and its crew members with 
regard to flight and duty time limitations and rest requirements for crew members.

### ORO.FTL.105 — Full Definitions

**(1) Acclimatised**: Circadian clock synchronized to time zone. Considered acclimatised 
within a 2-hour wide time zone band around local time at point of departure. When local 
time at next duty start differs by >2h, acclimatisation state determined by Table 1.

**(2) Reference time**: Local time at reporting point within the 2h time zone band where 
crew member is acclimatised.

**(3) Accommodation** (standby/split duty): Quiet, comfortable, not public, controllable 
light/temperature, adequate furniture for sleeping, sufficient capacity for all crew, 
food and drink access.

**(4) Suitable accommodation** (standby/split duty/rest): Separate room per crew member, 
quiet environment, bed, ventilation, temperature/light control, food and drink access.

**(5) Augmented flight crew**: More than minimum required, allowing each FCM to leave post 
for in-flight rest, replaced by appropriately qualified FCM.

**(6) Break**: Period within FDP, shorter than rest, free of all tasks, counts as duty.

**(7) Delayed reporting**: Postponement of scheduled FDP before crew member leaves rest place.

**(8) Disruptive schedule**: Roster disrupting sleep during optimal window.
- **Early type** (determined by competent authority): 
  - Early start: 05:00–05:59 acclimatised time
  - Late finish: 23:00–01:59 acclimatised time
- **Late type** (determined by competent authority):
  - Early start: 05:00–06:59 acclimatised time  
  - Late finish: 00:00–01:59 acclimatised time

**(9) Night duty**: Duty encroaching any portion of 02:00–04:59 acclimatised time.

**(10) Duty**: Any task for the operator: flight duty, admin, training, checking, positioning, 
some standby elements.

**(11) Duty period**: Starts when required to report for/commence duty. Ends when free of 
all duties including post-flight duty.

**(12) FDP (Flight Duty Period)**: Commences at reporting for duty that includes sector(s). 
Finishes when aircraft comes to rest and engines shut down at end of last operated sector.

**(13) Flight time**: Time from first aircraft movement from parking for takeoff until rest 
on designated parking with all engines shut down (block-to-block).

**(14) Home base**: Assigned location where crew normally starts/ends duty. Operator not 
responsible for accommodation under normal circumstances.

**(15) Local day**: 24h period from 00:00 local.

**(16) Local night**: 8h period between 22:00 and 08:00 local.

**(17) Operating crew member**: Crew member carrying out duties in aircraft during a sector.

**(18) Positioning**: Transfer of non-operating crew member at operator's behest. Excludes: 
private rest → reporting point travel, and local rest → duty start travel.

**(19) Rest facility**: 
- **Class 1**: Bunk or surface allowing flat/near-flat sleeping (≥80° recline from vertical). 
  Separate from cockpit AND passenger cabin. Controllable light. Isolated from noise/disturbance.
- **Class 2**: Seat in cabin, ≥45° recline, ≥55" (137.5cm) pitch, ≥20" (50cm) width, 
  leg/foot support. Separated from passengers by curtain minimum. Reasonably free from disturbance.
- **Class 3**: Seat in cabin or cockpit, ≥40° recline, leg/foot support. Separated by 
  curtain minimum. Not adjacent to passenger-occupied seat.

**(20) Reserve**: Period requiring availability for assignment, notified ≥10h in advance.

**(21) Rest period**: Continuous, uninterrupted, defined. Free of all duties, standby, reserve.

**(22) Rotation**: Duty/duties with ≥1 flight duty + rest out of home base, starting and 
ending at home base for rest where operator no longer responsible for accommodation.

**(23) Single day free of duty**: 1 day + 2 local nights, free of all duties and standby, 
notified in advance. Rest period may be included as part of it.

**(24) Sector**: Segment of FDP from first movement for takeoff to rest after landing on 
designated parking.

**(25) Standby**: Pre-notified, defined period. Must be available for flight, positioning, 
or other duty assignment without intervening rest.

**(26) Airport standby**: Standby performed at the airport.

**(27) Other standby**: Standby at home or in suitable accommodation.

**(28) WOCL (Window of Circadian Low)**: 02:00–05:59 acclimatised time.

## 2. Acclimatisation (Table 1)

### ORO.FTL.105(1) — Acclimatisation State Determination

| Time zone difference (h) between reference time and local time at next duty | <48h elapsed | 48–71:59h | 72–95:59h | 96–119:59h | ≥120h |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **<4h** | B | D | D | D | D |
| **≤6h** | B | X | D | D | D |
| **≤9h** | B | X | X | D | D |
| **≤12h** | B | X | X | X | D |

**States**:
- **B** = Acclimatised to local time of departure time zone → use Table 2 with departure reference time
- **D** = Acclimatised to local time at next duty start → use Table 2 with destination reference time
- **X** = Unknown state of acclimatisation → use Table 3 (or Table 4 with FRM)

**Time elapsed** = time since reporting at reference time.

**For rotations with ≥3 FDPs**: use the greatest time zone difference from original reference 
time. If crossing time zones in both directions, use highest number crossed in any single FDP.

### ORO.FTL.205(c) — Cabin Crew Different Reporting Time
When cabin crew needs earlier reporting for pre-flight briefing:
- FDP of cabin crew may be extended by the reporting time difference (max **1 hour**)
- Maximum daily FDP for cabin crew is based on **flight crew** reporting time
- FDP **starts** at the cabin crew's earlier reporting time

### ORO.FTL.205(d) — Extension Without In-Flight Rest

**(1)** Basic FDP may be extended by up to **1 hour**, not more than **twice in any 7 consecutive days**.
Rest compensation (one of):
- (i) Pre-flight AND post-flight rest each increased by **2 hours**; OR
- (ii) Post-flight rest increased by **4 hours**

**(2)** For consecutive extended FDPs: additional rest provided consecutively.

**(3)** Must be planned in advance. Limited to:
- (i) ≤5 sectors when WOCL is NOT encroached
- (ii) ≤4 sectors when WOCL encroached ≤2 hours
- (iii) ≤2 sectors when WOCL encroached >2 hours

**(4)** Cannot combine with in-flight rest or split duty extensions.

#### CS FTL.1.205(a) — Night Duty Provisions
(1) For consecutive night duties: max **4 sectors per duty**.
(2) Operator must apply appropriate fatigue risk management for night duties >10 hours 
regarding surrounding duties and rest periods.

### ORO.FTL.205(f) — Commander's Discretion (Unforeseen Circumstances)

Applies only to unforeseen circumstances starting **at or after** reporting time.

**(1)** Conditions:
- (i) Max FDP increased by ≤**2 hours** (non-augmented); ≤**3 hours** (augmented crew)
- (ii) If final sector exceeds allowed increase after takeoff: may continue to destination/alternate
- (iii) Subsequent rest may be reduced but **never less than 10 hours**

**(2)** If unforeseen circumstances could lead to severe fatigue: commander SHALL reduce FDP 
and/or increase rest to eliminate detrimental effect on safety.

**(3)** Commander must consult all crew members on alertness before deciding.

**(4)** Commander submits report to operator for any FDP increase or rest reduction.

**(5)** If increase exceeds 1 hour: operator sends report with comments to authority within 28 days.

**(6)** Operator implements non-punitive process, described in operations manual.

### ORO.FTL.205(g) — Delayed Reporting
Operator establishes procedures in OM per applicable certification specifications.

## 4. In-Flight Rest — Augmented Crew

### CS FTL.1.205(c) — Extension Due to In-Flight Rest

#### General conditions (CS FTL.1.205(c)(1)):
- FDP limited to **3 sectors**
- Minimum in-flight rest: consecutive **90 minutes** per crew member, and consecutive **2 hours** 
  for the flight crew members at control during landing
- All time in rest facility counts as FDP
- Minimum rest at destination: max(preceding duty period, **14 hours**)
- Crew member cannot start positioning to join this operating crew on same flight
- In-flight rest should be taken during cruise phase

#### Maximum FDP — Flight Crew (CS FTL.1.205(c)(2)):

| Augmentation | Class 3 | Class 2 | Class 1 |
|-------------|:-------:|:-------:|:-------:|
| **+1 additional FCM** | 14:00 | 15:00 | 16:00 |
| **+2 additional FCM** | 15:00 | 16:00 | 17:00 |

#### Long-haul bonus (CS FTL.1.205(c)(4)):
Limits above may be increased by **+1 hour** if:
- FDP includes **≤2 sectors** AND
- **1 sector** has >9 hours continuous flight time

This gives an absolute maximum of **18:00** (2 extra FCM + Class 1 + long-haul bonus).

#### Minimum In-Flight Rest — Cabin Crew (CS FTL.1.205(c)(3)):

| Max Extended FDP | Class 1 | Class 2 | Class 3 |
|-----------------|:-------:|:-------:|:-------:|
| up to 14:30 | 1:30 | 1:30 | 1:30 |
| 14:31–15:00 | 1:45 | 2:00 | 2:20 |
| 15:01–15:30 | 2:00 | 2:20 | 2:40 |
| 15:31–16:00 | 2:15 | 2:40 | 3:00 |
| 16:01–16:30 | 2:35 | 3:00 | N/A |
| 16:31–17:00 | 3:00 | 3:25 | N/A |
| 17:01–17:30 | 3:25 | N/A | N/A |
| 17:31–18:00 | 3:50 | N/A | N/A |

**Critical**: If ALL sectors are augmented in an FDP, they ALL must be augmented — 
cannot mix augmented and non-augmented sectors in the same FDP.

## 5. Delayed Reporting

### CS FTL.1.205(d) — Algorithm

Operator may delay reporting in unforeseen circumstances. Procedures in OM. Records kept.

**(1)** One notification of delay → calculate per (iii) or (iv) below.

**(2)** If reporting further amended after first delay: FDP starts counting **1 hour after 
second notification** OR at **original delayed reporting time**, whichever is earlier.

**(3)** Delay **<4 hours**: Max FDP based on **original** reporting time. FDP counts from 
**delayed** reporting time.

**(4)** Delay **≥4 hours**: Max FDP based on **more limiting** of original or delayed 
reporting time. FDP counts from **delayed** reporting time.

**(5)** Exception: Delay of **≥10 hours** with no further disturbance → counts as rest period.

## 7. Split Duty

### ORO.FTL.220 + CS FTL.1.220

**(a)** Break on ground within FDP: minimum **3 consecutive hours** duration.

**(b)** FDP extension = **50%** of the break time, subject to:
- Time of break exceeding **6 hours** does NOT count for extension
- Time of break encroaching **WOCL** does NOT count for extension

The break excludes time for post and pre-flight duties and travelling. Minimum total 
time for post and pre-flight duties and travelling: **30 minutes**. Operator specifies 
actual times in OM.

**(c)** Break counts in full as FDP.

**(d)** Split duty cannot follow reduced rest.

**(e)** Split duty cannot combine with in-flight rest extensions.

**Facility requirements**: 
- Break <6h: accommodation (quiet, comfortable, ability to sleep)
- Break ≥6h: suitable accommodation (separate room, bed)

## 8. Standby

### ORO.FTL.225 + CS FTL.1.225

#### (a) Airport Standby (ASB)

- Counts **in full** as duty period for ORO.FTL.210 and ORO.FTL.235
- Start/end time in roster, notified in advance
- Operator provides accommodation
- If ASB does NOT lead to FDP: followed by rest per ORO.FTL.235

**If FDP assigned during ASB** (CS FTL.1.225(a)(2)):
- FDP counts from start of the **FDP** (not ASB)
- Max FDP reduced by ASB time in excess of **4 hours**
- Max combined ASB + FDP (per ORO.FTL.205(b)+(d)): **16 hours**

#### (b) Other Standby (Home Standby) — CS FTL.1.225(b)

**(1)** Maximum duration: **16 hours**

**(2)** Procedures designed so combination of standby + FDP ≤ **18 hours awake time**

**(3)** **25%** of time on other standby counts as duty for ORO.FTL.210

**(4)** Followed by rest per ORO.FTL.235

**(5)** Standby ceases when crew member reports at designated reporting point

**(6)** If standby ceases within first **6 hours**: max FDP counts from reporting

**(7)** If standby ceases after **6 hours**: max FDP reduced by amount of standby exceeding 6h

**(8)** If FDP is extended due to in-flight rest (CS FTL.1.205(c)) or split duty (CS FTL.1.220): 
the 6h threshold in (6) and (7) becomes **8 hours**

**(9)** If standby starts between **23:00 and 07:00**: time in that window does NOT count towards 
FDP reduction under (6), (7), (8) until crew member is contacted by operator

**(10)** Response time between call and reporting allows reasonable travel from rest place

## 9. Reserve

### ORO.FTL.230 + CS FTL.1.230

**(a)** Reserve in roster.

**(b)** Flight time specification schemes specify:
- (1) Maximum duration of any single reserve period
- (2) Maximum consecutive reserve days

**CS FTL.1.230**:
- Minimum notification time from being notified until required to report for FDP, ASB, or 
  start OSB: **10 hours**
- Reserve times do **NOT** count as duty period for ORO.FTL.210 or ORO.FTL.235
- Crew member on reserve must meet rest requirements for extended recovery rest (ORO.FTL.235(d))

## 10. Rest Periods

### ORO.FTL.235 + CS FTL.1.235

#### (a) Minimum Rest at Home Base

**(1)** Before an FDP starting at home base:
```
min_rest = max(preceding_duty_period, 12 hours)
```

**(2)** By derogation from (1): if operator provides **suitable accommodation** at home base, 
the away-from-base minimum under (b) may apply.

#### (b) Minimum Rest Away from Home Base

```
min_rest = max(preceding_duty_period, 10 hours)
```
Must include **8-hour sleep opportunity** in addition to time for travelling and 
physiological needs. Time for physiological needs should be at least **1 hour**.

#### (c) Reduced Rest (by derogation, requires FRM)

Flight time specification schemes may reduce minimum rest, considering:
- (1) Minimum reduced rest period (never below **10 hours** at home base per CS FTL.1.235)
- (2) Increase of subsequent rest period (compensatory rest)
- (3) Reduction of FDP following the reduced rest

CS FTL.1.235(b):
- **(1)** Reduced rest may not be below **10 hours**
- **(2)** Subsequent rest must be increased by the difference between actual rest and 
  the required rest per ORO.FTL.235(a)/(b)
- **(3)** Reduced rest may not be used more than **twice** between two extended recovery rest periods
- **(4)** FDP following reduced rest must be reduced by the same amount as the rest reduction
- **(5)** Reduced rest must not follow an FDP where the operator used the extension under 
  ORO.FTL.205(d) (extension without in-flight rest)

#### (d) Recurrent Extended Recovery Rest

```
minimum_duration = 36 hours, including 2 local nights (22:00–08:00)
max_gap_between_extended_rests = 168 hours (7 days)
```

Twice per month: increased to **2 local days**.

#### (e) Additional Rest for Time Zone Compensation
Flight time specification schemes specify additional rest per applicable CS to compensate for:
- (1) Effects of time zone differences
- (2) Effects of extensions of the FDP

#### CS FTL.1.235(b)(3) — Rotation Rest
For rotations involving time zone crossings ≥4h:
- Additional local nights of rest at home base before undertaking another FDP
- Number depends on time zone difference and rotation duration
- Detailed in GM1 and GM2 CS FTL.1.235(b)(3)

#### Disruptive Schedule Rest (CS FTL.1.235(a))
If transition from late finish/night duty to early start is planned at home base:
- Rest period between them must include **2 local nights**
- Competent authority determines early/late type per ARO.OPS.230

## 11. Positioning

### ORO.FTL.215

**(a)** Positioning after reporting but prior to operating: counts as **FDP** but NOT as a sector.

**(b)** All time spent on positioning counts as **duty period**.

A crew member on positioning is not operating → positioning is not a sector for FDP table 
lookup purposes. But positioning time is included in the FDP duration for max FDP calculation.

## 12. Operator Responsibilities

### ORO.FTL.110

The operator shall:
- **(a)** Publish duty rosters sufficiently in advance for crew to plan adequate rest
- **(b)** Plan FDPs enabling crew to remain sufficiently free from fatigue
- **(c)** Specify reporting times with sufficient time for ground duties
- **(d)** Consider cumulative effects of long duty hours + minimum rest
- **(e)** Avoid practices disrupting established sleep/work patterns (alternating day/night)
- **(f)** Comply with disruptive schedule provisions per ARO.OPS.230
- **(g)** Provide rest periods sufficient to overcome previous duties
- **(h)** Plan and notify extended recovery rest in advance
- **(i)** Plan flight duties completable within allowable FDP including pre-flight, sector, turnaround
- **(j)** Change schedule/crew if actual exceeds max FDP on **>33%** of flight duties in seasonal period

### ORO.FTL.115 — Crew Member Responsibilities
- (a) Comply with CAT.GEN.MPA.100(b) (fitness for duty)
- (b) Make optimum use of rest opportunities and facilities

## 13. FRM, Records, Nutrition, Training

### ORO.FTL.120 — Fatigue Risk Management (FRM)

Required when using deviations from CS FTL.1 or when specified by the Subpart.
Integrated in operator's SMS. Described in operations manual. Must include:
1. FRM policy
2. Documentation of processes
3. Scientific principles and knowledge
4. Continuous hazard identification and risk assessment
5. Risk mitigation with remedial actions, monitoring, and assessment
6. FRM safety assurance processes
7. FRM promotion processes

Must correspond to flight time specification scheme, operator size, and nature/complexity 
of activities.

### ORO.FTL.125 — Flight Time Specification Schemes

- Must be appropriate for type(s) of operation
- Must comply with Regulation 216/2008 and this Subpart
- Must be **approved by competent authority** before implementation
- Deviations from CS FTL.1: require full description, manual revisions, compliance assessment
- Within **2 years** of deviation implementation: operator collects and analyses data on 
  fatigue effects, provides report to competent authority

### ORO.FTL.240 — Nutrition
- **(a)** Operator provides opportunity for meals and drinks during FDP
- **(b)** At least one meal for FDP exceeding **6 hours**

### ORO.FTL.245 — Records
Individual records for each crew member, maintained for **24 months**:
- Flight times
- Start, duration, and end of each duty period
- Rest periods and days free of duty

Training and checking records: retained for **3 years** (per ORO.MLR.115).

### ORO.FTL.250 — Fatigue Management Training
- Initial and recurrent training for:
  - Crew members
  - Personnel responsible for roster preparation/maintenance
  - Management personnel concerned
- Training programme established by operator, described in OM
- Syllabus covers causes and effects of fatigue, countermeasures, shared responsibilities
